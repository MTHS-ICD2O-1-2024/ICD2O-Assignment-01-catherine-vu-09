Donald Knuth full name is Donald Ervin Knuth and he was born on January 10, 1938. Donald was born in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. He is an only child. He is an American computer scientist and mathematician. He demonstrated his problem solving skills at school. In middle school, he won a word contest by identifying over 4,500 words from "Ziegler's Giant Bar, “ which made them win a whole television and candy bars for everyone.
Education
In 1956, he had a scholarship to Case Institute of Technology in 1956, taking his first interest into computing. During his scholarship, he already had quite a few accomplishments, by rewriting IBM 650's code. 2 years later, creating a program to observe basketball player performance, he helped assemble Case's Engineering and Science Review, winning a national award a year later. Then switching his course, mathematics, earned both a bachelor's and master's degree another year after. He wrote an ALGOL compiler for Burroughs Corporation. Finally, earning his PhD in mathematics from Caltech in 1963.
Work
In 1967, he created the term Analysis of Algorithms at a SIAM conference. Then working at Princeton, he joined Stanford in 1969, becoming a professor because of cryptographic research. A few decades later, he earned emeritus status in 1993.
