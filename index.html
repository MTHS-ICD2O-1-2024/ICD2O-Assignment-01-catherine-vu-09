About Him

Knuth's full name is Donald Ervin Knuth and he was born on January 10, 1938. Donald was born in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. He is an only child. He is an American computer scientist and mathematician. He demonstrated his problem solving skills at school. In middle school, he won a word contest by identifying over 4,500 words

Education

In 1956, he had a scholarship to Case Institute of Technology in 1956, taking his first interest in computing. During his scholarship, he already had quite a few accomplishments, by rewriting IBM 650's code. 2 years later, creating a program to observe basketball player performance, he helped assemble Case's Engineering and Science Review, winning a national award a year later. Then switching his course, mathematics, earned both a bachelor's and master's degree another year after. He wrote an ALGOL compiler for Burroughs Corporation. Finally, earning his PhD in mathematics from Caltech in 1963.
